Bacteria

What are bacteria?

Bacteria are microscopic single-celled organisms that are vital to our ecosystems.

Your kit tests for coliform and E. coli which normally live in the digestive tract and feces of humans and animals. Sewage overflows, water main breaks, and other environmental factors can cause bacterial contamination in drinking water.

Coliform bacteria are not typically harmful, but their presence can be indication of something more dangerous.

E. coli is a species of coliform bacteria that can cause serious illness if ingested.

How will I know if my water is unsafe?
  • No visible sign
  • No distinct taste
  • No distinct odor

Only a test can confirm contamination.

Your public water system is required to notify you if your drinking water is unsafe, but privately-owned, individual water supply safety rests in the hands of its owner.

What are the potential health risks?

E. coli outbreaks in water systems are rare, but exposure can have adverse health effects.

  • vomiting
  • stomach issues
  • hepatitis
  • fever
  • gastrointestinal issues

Copper

What is copper?

Copper is an essential nutrient but toxic at high levels. It's used in coins, electrical wiring, and water pipes. Corrosion of copper plumbing materials can cause water contamination.

Iron

What is iron?

Iron occurs naturally throughout the environment and is essential for the transport of oxygen in the body. Iron is common in drinking water and can enter through natural sources or through rusting water fixtures and pipes.

How will I know if my water is unsafe?

  • Metallic taste
  • Unpleasant odor
What are the potential health risks?

Ingesting water with iron is not directly associated with health problems, however iron sediment can harbor bacteria which may cause adverse health effects.

Nitrite / Nitrate

What are nitrites/nitrates?

Nitrates/nitrites are nitrogen-oxygen compounds essential for plant growth. Excess accumulations of nitrates/nitrites can enter ground water through fertilizer, animal waste, and septic tank leakage, and cause contamination.

pH

What is pH?

pH measures how acidic/alkaline your water is on a scale of 0 to 14. Water with a pH below 7 is considered acidic and may contain elevated levels of toxic metals. Water with a pH above 7 is considered alkaline and generally associated with an abundance of calcium and/or magnesium.

How will I know if my water is unsafe?

    Above 7pH - deposits on dishes and pipes/fixtures
  • Below 7pH - metallic or sour taste
    Above 7pH - deposits on dishes and pipes/fixtures
  • Below 7pH - metallic odor
    Above 7pH - no distinct odor

A pH above 8.5 may indicate hard water which can decrease the efficiency of electric water heaters. This can increase detergent and hot water use, raising costs for you and your family.

Only a test can confirm contamination.

What are the potential health risks?

Water with a high pH has not been shown to cause health problems. However, if you have heart or circulatory problems, consult your physician before lowering the pH of your water as the process involves the addition of sodium.

Low pH water may indicate an abundance of metal ions which can cause adverse health effects.

Hardness

What is hardness?

Hardness refers to the concentration of dissolved minerals in your water. It varies depending on geographic location and water source. Water is too hard when large amounts of dissolved calcium, magnesium, and other minerals are present.

How will I know if my water is unsafe?
  • Deposits on dishes, build-up in pipes/appliances, dull hair, stiff laundry
  • Bitter taste
  • Unpleasant odor

Hard water can be harmful to your pipes and decrease the efficiency of electric water heaters. This can increase detergent and hot water use, raising costs for you and your family.

Only a test can confirm contamination.

What are the potential health risks?

Hardness doesn't have known health effects. In fact, water containing calcium and magnesium can help you reach the average daily requirements for these minerals.

What should I do if my test reveals unsafe levels?

Hard water is more of a nuisance than a cause for alarm. If your experiencing buildup on fixtures and poor soap and detergent performance, schedule a complimentary in-home test with your local Culligan expert. They can discuss concentration levels with you and make recommendations for treatment.
